Plant Information


Plant Name:Rain-Tree


Benefits: The Rain Tree is highly valued for its numerous environmental, medicinal, and practical benefits. Known for its large, umbrella-shaped canopy, it provides excellent shade, making it a popular choice for urban areas and parks, where it helps lower temperatures and improves air quality. Additionally, its leaves fold at night and during rainfall, allowing sunlight to reach the ground for other vegetation to thrive, thus contributing to biodiversity. Medicinally, parts of the Rain Tree are used in traditional remedies, as its bark and leaves contain properties that help treat ailments like colds, coughs, and skin irritations. The tree also benefits the soil by fixing nitrogen, thereby enriching it and supporting healthier plant growth around it.



Season: The Rain Tree is evergreen in tropical climates, meaning it retains its lush foliage year-round in areas without a marked dry season. However, in regions with a pronounced dry season, it may shed its leaves briefly. It flowers abundantly in warm, wet seasons, producing fluffy, pink-and-white blossoms that attract pollinators like bees, butterflies, and birds. Flowering often occurs just before the rainy season or at the beginning of it, which varies by region but typically spans from late spring to early fall in tropical and subtropical zones.



Region: Originally native to Central and South America, the Rain Tree has been widely cultivated and naturalized in tropical and subtropical regions around the world. It thrives in Southeast Asia, the Indian subcontinent, the Caribbean, and various parts of Africa, due to its adaptability to diverse climates and soil types. This hardy species prefers warm, humid environments with ample rainfall but can tolerate brief periods of drought. Its resilience and adaptability have led to its popularity as an ornamental and shade-providing tree in cities and along roadsides globally.



Age: The Rain Tree is a long-lived species, often reaching an impressive age when given the right conditions. It typically lives between 80 to 100 years, although some specimens may exceed this lifespan, especially in undisturbed natural settings. This longevity, combined with its rapid growth rate, makes it an excellent choice for providing long-term benefits in both natural and urban environments. Its strong, expansive root system and durable trunk contribute to its ability to withstand storms and other environmental stresses over the decades.
